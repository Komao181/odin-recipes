Gotong Batangas Recipe

Description

Gotong Batangas is a rich and flavorful beef stew that hails from Batangas, a province in the Philippines. Unlike traditional rice-based "goto," this version is all about the beefy goodness, using various parts of the cow, such as tripe and innards, simmered in a savory broth. The dish is hearty and comforting, often enjoyed with a side of rice and spiced with chili for an extra kick.

This dish is perfect for those who love a bold, meaty flavor and appreciate traditional Filipino comfort food with a regional twist.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef tripe, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 500g beef innards (liver, heart, kidney), cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 6 cups beef broth
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat and saut√© the garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add the beef tripe and innards, then cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in the beef broth and add the bay leaves. Bring to a boil.
  4. Lower the heat and simmer for 1-2 hours until the tripe is tender and the broth is rich in flavor.
  5. Season with fish sauce, ground black pepper, and salt to taste. Simmer for another 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice and garnish with chopped chili or calamansi, if desired.
