Batil Patong Recipe

Description

Batil Patong is a beloved noodle dish from Tuguegarao, Cagayan, in the northern Philippines. This hearty and savory stir-fried noodle dish features a combination of sautéed ground beef, pork liver, vegetables, and topped with a fried egg. It’s served with a rich soup made from the same broth used to cook the noodles, and is often accompanied by a side of calamansi or vinegar for a tangy kick.

Batil Patong is a deliciously satisfying meal that’s perfect for any time of the day, combining robust flavors with the comfort of noodles.

Ingredients

  • 250g miki noodles (fresh egg noodles)
  • 200g ground beef
  • 100g pork liver, sliced thinly
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 eggs (1 for topping, 1 for mixing with the broth)
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat, then sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add ground beef and pork liver, cooking until browned and fully cooked. Season with soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, and pepper.
  3. In a separate pot, cook the miki noodles in beef broth for a few minutes until tender. Drain and set aside, reserving the broth.
  4. Fry one egg sunny-side up and set it aside for topping.
  5. Mix one beaten egg into the reserved beef broth to create the "batil" or scrambled egg soup.
  6. Place the cooked noodles on a plate, top with the meat mixture, and the fried egg ("patong").
  7. Serve the noodles with the egg-drop soup on the side and garnish with calamansi or vinegar, if desired.
